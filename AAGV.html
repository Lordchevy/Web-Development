Gun Violence In America

There continually exists the danger of gun violence anywhere we go, including movie theaters, houses of worship, concerts, schools, and shopping centers. These days, when someone brings up a mass shooting, one would calmly ask, "Which one?" The United States has one of the highest rates of gun-related violence among developed countries. According to data from the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC), firearms are involved in tens of thousands of deaths and injuries each year.

The high rate of gun homicides in the United States raises important questions about the effectiveness of current policies and the need for comprehensive strategies to reduce gun violence.

Impact On The Children

Gun Violence doesn't discriminate. It affects all kinds of people in all parts of our nation. However, the impact on many areas is disproportionately high. Gun violence in America disproportionately impacts certain groups, exacerbating existing social inequalities and injustices. While no demographic is immune to its effects, several groups bear a disproportionate burden. Children and adolescents are unfortunately one of these groups. In various ways, from witnessing shootings in their communities to being victims of accidental shootings or school shootings. Exposure to gun violence can have profound psychological effects on young people, leading to trauma, anxiety, and long-term emotional distress.


Sign Our Petition!

In our neighborhoods, gun violence has spread like wildfire, taking countless lives and shattering families' lives apart. It's time for us to demand that our politicians do something about this senseless violence. Innocent people lose their lives to gun violence every day, whether as a result of accidents, homicides, suicides, or mass shootings. This cannot be accepted as normal going forward.

Our Petition: We, Americans Against Gun Violence, call on lawmakers at every level of government to enact meaningful reforms to address gun violence in our society. We demand action to:

1. We urge lawmakers to implement comprehensive background checks for all gun purchases, close loopholes that allow dangerous individuals to access firearms, and ban the sale of military-style assault weapons and high-capacity magazines.

2. We recognize the connection between mental health issues and gun violence. We call for increased funding for mental health services and support programs to help prevent individuals in crisis from accessing firearms.

3. We advocate for education and training programs to promote responsible gun ownership, including safe storage practices to prevent accidents and unauthorized access to firearms.

4. We support community-based violence prevention initiatives, including programs that address root causes of violence such as poverty, inequality, and lack of access to resources and opportunities.
